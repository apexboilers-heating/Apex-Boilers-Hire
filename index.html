Emergency Boiler Hire for Heating Failure

Boiler failures happen without warning. When a primary heating plant fails in a school, hospital, care home, or commercial building, the impact can be serious — particularly in winter months or where vulnerable people are present.

Apex Boiler Hire provides emergency boiler hire to restore temporary heating and hot water to your site with minimum delay. We operate a 24/7 response service and maintain a fleet of hire-ready equipment for commercial and industrial sites across the UK.

Typical Emergency Hire Scenarios

  • Primary boiler failure with no backup capacity
  • Heat exchanger or pressure vessel failure requiring plant shutdown
  • Controls or burner failure awaiting parts or specialist repair
  • Fire, flood, or mechanical damage to plant room equipment
  • Failure of boiler during extreme cold weather
  • Urgent loss of domestic hot water supply
  • Plant corrosion or sludge failure requiring emergency isolation

Who We Support in Emergencies

  • Schools and academies — protecting pupils, staff, and exam timetables
  • NHS buildings and hospitals — maintaining critical heating in clinical areas
  • Care homes and residential facilities — protecting vulnerable occupants
  • Local authority buildings and community centres
  • Commercial offices, hotels, and leisure facilities
  • Industrial and manufacturing sites requiring process heat or heating
  • Housing associations and social landlords

The Emergency Boiler Hire Process

Step 1 — Call us immediately. Our team will take details of your site, building type, heating load, and urgency. We'll ask about available connection points, access, and fuel supply.

Step 2 — We confirm availability and mobilise. We identify the most suitable unit from our hire fleet, confirm delivery logistics, and dispatch equipment and engineers.

Step 3 — Delivery and installation. Our engineers arrive with the temporary boiler and associated equipment. We connect to your existing pipework, install flue systems, and commission the unit.

Step 4 — Handover and support. Once commissioned, we brief your site team on operation and remain available for support throughout the hire period.

Nationwide Emergency Response

We understand that every hour without heating in a critical building carries a cost — operational, reputational, and in some cases welfare-related. Our emergency response is structured to deliver equipment to site and achieve commissioning as quickly as logistics permit.

In many cases, we can achieve same-day or next-morning delivery for sites within our logistics network. Response time will depend on your location, site access requirements, and the complexity of connection. For non-urgent programmes, our planned temporary heating service helps reduce shutdown risk before a failure occurs.

Tip: Keep a record of your primary boiler's output (kW or BTU), fuel type, and main flow/return connection sizes. This information helps us specify the correct hire unit and saves valuable time during an emergency call-out.

Planned Temporary Heating Solutions

Not every temporary boiler hire requirement is an emergency. Many of our clients use Apex Boiler Hire as part of a well-planned capital works or maintenance programme — providing continuity of heating and hot water while permanent systems are upgraded, replaced, or refurbished.

Planning ahead allows us to ensure the right equipment is correctly specified, logistics are agreed in advance, and the installation is completed before the existing plant goes offline. This approach results in a smoother project with minimal disruption to the building's occupants and is ideal for planned shutdown heating, plant room upgrades, and boiler replacement programmes.

Typical Planned Hire Scenarios

  • Planned boiler replacement — temporary cover while the permanent plant is removed and new equipment installed
  • School summer or holiday shutdown programmes — heating system overhaul with temporary cover if works overrun
  • Hospital and healthcare capital programmes — maintaining LTHW and DHW to wards and clinical areas
  • Care home upgrades — no interruption to heating for vulnerable residents during works
  • Commercial refurbishment — temporary heating plant supporting phased plant room upgrades
